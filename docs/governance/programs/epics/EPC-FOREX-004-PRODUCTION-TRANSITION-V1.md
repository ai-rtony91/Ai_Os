## 22H/6D Supervised Forex Operations Doctrine

This doctrine augments EPC-FOREX-004 as the production-transition planning layer for 22H/6D supervised Forex operations. It remains subordinate to `AGENTS.md`, `RISK_POLICY.md`, PRG-FOREX-001, the official Buckets above, and every protected-action gate.

This section creates no duplicate EPC-FOREX-004 authority. Its buckets are operating doctrine buckets, not promoted Program Buckets. Its packet list is a candidate queue, not executable approval. Any candidate must still arrive as a complete governed packet with identity chain, mode, zone, lane, allowed paths, forbidden paths, approval authority, validator chain, stop point, and Human Owner approval where required.

### 22H/6D Operating Target

The 22H/6D target is a supervised demo-readiness operating goal: AIOS should be able to support up to 22 hours of monitored Forex operating coverage per supervised operating day across a six-day evidence window without hidden live-trading authority, unbounded automation, or unmanaged failure states.

The target is not a promise of live deployment, profit, account growth, broker availability, or continuous production operation. It is a readiness and evidence standard for governed observation, candidate discovery, risk review, demo-intent preparation, trade-management planning, broker-health review, audit capture, and Human Owner decision support.

For EPC-FOREX-004, 22H/6D evidence is acceptable only when it is:

- supervised by an approved human review path.
- bounded to paper, simulated, replay, or separately approved demo-only workflows.
- recorded with enough evidence to reconstruct decisions, timing, assumptions, risk limits, and stop conditions.
- blocked from live trading, broker credential use, production activation, scheduler creation, daemon creation, webhook activation, and money movement unless a future higher-authority packet explicitly approves the narrow action.

### Supervised Autonomy Definition

Supervised autonomy means AIOS may prepare, rank, monitor, reconcile, and explain Forex operational decisions inside an approved packet boundary while a Human Owner retains approval authority over protected actions, broker access, credential use, live trading, production transition, and money movement.

In this doctrine, AIOS may be designed to:

- observe market and session context.
- identify and score candidate setups.
- calculate proposed risk and position-sizing scenarios.
- draft demo-intent records and management plans.
- watch evidence freshness and dashboard truth.
- surface stop conditions and escalation reasons.

Supervised autonomy does not mean AIOS may silently place trades, route orders, access credentials, call live endpoints, create always-on schedulers, run background daemons, modify runtime code, bypass validators, bypass Human Owner approval, or convert demo evidence into live authority.

### Persistent Profitability Doctrine

Persistent profitability is an evidence threshold, not a slogan or an assumption. AIOS must not claim persistent profitability from one run, one favorable regime, one manual backtest, one paper account sequence, or one 22H/6D readiness window.

Persistent profitability evidence must be:

- net of spread, commissions, slippage assumptions, financing where applicable, and rejected or missed execution cases.
- evaluated across different sessions, pairs, volatility regimes, news conditions, and drawdown periods.
- compared against defined risk budgets, maximum adverse excursion, maximum favorable excursion, hold-time rules, and stop conditions.
- reproducible from stored evidence without relying on private memory, screenshots alone, or unverified dashboard state.
- separated from live-trading approval unless `RISK_POLICY.md` and Human Owner approval explicitly authorize a future exception.

The 22H/6D doctrine can produce readiness evidence for persistent profitability review. It cannot, by itself, certify persistent profitability or authorize live trading.

### Doctrine Bucket 1: Market Awareness

Purpose: maintain a governed view of market regime, session state, calendar risk, pair behavior, volatility, spread conditions, and unavailable or unsafe trading windows.

Required outputs for future packets:

- market-regime snapshot format.
- pair and session coverage matrix.
- news and event-risk exclusion rules.
- stale-data and conflicting-source stop conditions.
- evidence fields needed to explain why a setup was considered or rejected.

Safety boundary: market awareness may inform scoring and readiness only. It does not authorize order placement, broker calls, live endpoints, or autonomous routing.

### Doctrine Bucket 2: Candidate Discovery

Purpose: discover, filter, and rank candidate Forex setups using governed criteria that can be audited after the fact.

Required outputs for future packets:

- candidate intake record.
- scoring rubric for trend, range, momentum, volatility, liquidity, and news risk.
- rejection reason taxonomy.
- duplicate-signal handling.
- handoff record from candidate discovery to risk review.

Safety boundary: candidate discovery produces candidate records only. A candidate is never an instruction to trade.

### Doctrine Bucket 3: Risk And Position Sizing

Purpose: convert candidate setups into bounded risk scenarios before any demo-intent or trade-management planning.

Required outputs for future packets:

- per-setup risk budget.
- maximum loss and drawdown impact.
- position-size calculation evidence.
- stop-loss, invalidation, and no-trade thresholds.
- risk-of-ruin and correlation checks when multiple candidates overlap.

Safety boundary: sizing calculations are decision-support artifacts. They do not authorize account access, order routing, leverage changes, or money movement.

### Doctrine Bucket 4: Governed Demo Execution

Purpose: define how a future approved packet may prepare and supervise demo-only execution workflows without creating live authority.

Required outputs for future packets:

- demo-intent approval card.
- pre-demo checklist.
- evidence capture before, during, and after demo activity.
- stop rules for data drift, spread expansion, calendar events, tool failure, or Human Owner uncertainty.
- explicit separation between demo activity and live trading.

Safety boundary: this bucket does not authorize demo order routing by itself. Any demo order workflow requires a separate packet that names the broker boundary, mode, allowed paths, validators, and Human Owner approval. Live trading remains blocked.

### Doctrine Bucket 5: Trade Management And Profit Protection

Purpose: define governed supervision for open demo or simulated positions after entry intent is approved by a future packet.

Required outputs for future packets:

- management plan for stop movement, partial exit scenarios, time-based review, invalidation, and profit protection.
- maximum adverse excursion and maximum favorable excursion evidence.
- rule for when AIOS must stop suggesting management changes and escalate.
- outcome record that separates process quality from profit or loss.

Safety boundary: trade-management planning cannot mutate live positions, call broker APIs, or manage money without separate higher-authority approval.

### Doctrine Bucket 6: Broker Health And Recovery

Purpose: define readiness evidence for broker connectivity, demo environment health, rate limits, rejected orders, stale prices, outages, login failure, and recovery behavior.

Required outputs for future packets:

- broker-health checklist.
- degraded-mode and stop-mode criteria.
- recovery drill plan.
- no-credential evidence boundary.
- incident report format for outages, rejected calls, or inconsistent broker state.

Safety boundary: broker-health planning must not read secrets, credentials, tokens, account IDs, vaults, or environment variables. Any broker interaction must be separately approved and demo-bounded unless a future live exception is granted under `RISK_POLICY.md`.

### Doctrine Bucket 7: Evidence, Audit, And Dashboard Truth

Purpose: make dashboard, report, and evidence state match the actual repo and runtime evidence instead of aspirational status.

Required outputs for future packets:

- canonical evidence fields.
- dashboard truth reconciliation checklist.
- audit trail for candidate, risk, demo-intent, management, and outcome records.
- stale, missing, contradictory, or unverifiable evidence stop conditions.
- morning/evening review record for supervised windows.

Safety boundary: dashboard truth work may report and reconcile evidence only. It does not authorize dashboard execution wiring, runtime mutation, production activation, or protected actions.

### Doctrine Bucket 8: Supervised Autonomy Readiness

Purpose: determine whether AIOS is ready to run longer supervised windows with clear stop controls and Human Owner visibility.

Required outputs for future packets:

- supervised autonomy readiness checklist.
- stop, pause, resume, and escalation rules.
- operator approval inbox or equivalent handoff record.
- bounded autonomy permissions matrix.
- evidence that every automated suggestion can be traced to source data and policy.

Safety boundary: readiness does not grant autonomy. It is evidence for a later Human Owner decision.

### Doctrine Bucket 9: Persistent Profitability Evaluation

Purpose: evaluate whether supervised Forex evidence is repeatable, risk-adjusted, and resilient enough to justify future readiness review.

Required outputs for future packets:

- profitability scorecard.
- after-cost performance view.
- drawdown and recovery analysis.
- regime coverage table.
- false-positive, missed-trade, and no-trade analysis.
- evidence threshold for continuing, pausing, simplifying, or replacing the strategy path.

Safety boundary: profitability evaluation is not live authority. Positive demo or simulated performance does not authorize live trading, micro-capital trading, broker credentials, production activation, or money movement.
