# AIOS Forex Store Listing Claims Policy V1

## Status

Internal product policy. This document is not public marketing copy, not legal advice, not store-submission approval, and not release approval.

AIOS Forex is not Play Store ready until final release review. AIOS Forex is not legally or commercially approved until owner legal/compliance review.

## Purpose

This policy defines how AIOS Forex may and may not describe itself in app titles, short descriptions, long descriptions, screenshots, videos, release notes, support copy, ads, and public product pages.

## Allowed Claims

Allowed claim categories must be evidence-backed and owner-reviewed:

- governed Forex automation support.
- human-controlled review workflow.
- paper simulation support when implemented.
- supervised demo evidence support when implemented.
- broker-readiness evidence review when sanitized.
- owner approval gates.
- risk controls.
- audit/evidence tracking.
- Vacation Mode control-plane status after source implementation.

Allowed claims must identify state precisely: planned, implemented, tested, paper-only, demo-only, metadata-only, owner-review required, or blocked.

## Forbidden Claims

The following phrases are prohibited claim examples and may appear only in policy discussion, not as public product claims:

- guaranteed profit
- guaranteed returns
- risk-free
- passive income guaranteed
- set and forget
- prints money
- fully autonomous live trading
- no loss possible
- legal compliant
- Play Store approved
- sell ready
- production deployed

Additional forbidden claim examples:

- hands-free income.
- AI prints money.
- effortless income.
- legally cleared trading.
- live profitable for every user.
- no review needed.
- no owner approval needed.
- broker action hidden from the user.

## Required Disclaimers

Public listing drafts must include owner-reviewed disclosures covering:

- Forex trading can lose money.
- Past evidence does not promise future outcomes.
- AIOS Forex is owner-governed.
- Live trading is blocked unless separately approved.
- The product is not investment advice unless later legal review approves exact wording.
- Support, escalation, shutdown, and risk controls must be visible.

## Evidence-Backed Claim Rule

No claim may be published unless it can be tied to:

- source implementation.
- validation output.
- sanitized evidence.
- owner review.
- release status.

Claims must distinguish broker-verified evidence from paper, demo, dry-run, simulated, reconciled, or projected states.

## Live-Proof Claim Boundary

AIOS Forex must not use "proven live profitable" unless broker-verified evidence exists, is sanitized, is reconciled, and owner/legal review approves exact public wording. Even then, the statement must not imply future outcome certainty.

## Store Description Constraints

Store description drafts must not:

- hide financial risk.
- hide owner approval requirements.
- imply live action without authority.
- imply credential storage.
- expose account identifiers.
- expose broker receipts or raw broker payloads.
- imply unnecessary permissions.
- omit support and shutdown paths.

## Screenshot And Video Claim Constraints

Screenshots and videos must not show:

- credentials.
- account identifiers.
- private broker receipts.
- raw broker payloads.
- private PnL evidence.
- unapproved live authority states.
- claims not backed by implemented source and validation.

## Marketing Review Gate

Marketing copy is blocked until owner review verifies:

- allowed claim category.
- evidence source.
- financial-risk disclosure.
- privacy/data-safety disclosure.
- live authority boundary.
- support/escalation language.
- no forbidden claim.

## Legal And Compliance Review Gate

Legal/compliance review is required before public launch, sale, store submission, paid promotion, financial-services claims, investment-advice claims, jurisdiction-specific copy, or user agreement publication.
