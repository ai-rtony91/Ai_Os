# AIOS Forex Financial Risk Disclosure Policy V1

## Status

Internal product policy. This document is not legal advice, not financial advice, not investment advice, and not release approval.

AIOS Forex is not Play Store ready until final release review. AIOS Forex is not legally or commercially approved until owner legal/compliance review.

## Purpose

This policy defines the minimum financial-risk disclosure posture for AIOS Forex before Vacation Mode control-plane implementation, mobile packaging, public claims, sale, or store submission.

## Financial-Risk Baseline

AIOS Forex must state that Forex trading can lose money. It must not imply outcome certainty, protected principal, fixed return outcomes, passive-income outcomes, or ownerless trading success.

Any user-facing experience must make risk visible before any trade-authority path. Readiness evidence, model output, broker receipts, historical results, and demo proof do not remove future risk.

## Forbidden Claims

The following phrases are prohibited claim examples and may appear only in policy discussion, not as public product claims:

- guaranteed profit
- guaranteed returns
- risk-free
- passive income guaranteed
- set and forget
- prints money
- fully autonomous live trading
- no loss possible
- legal compliant
- Play Store approved
- sell ready
- production deployed

## No Profit Guarantee

AIOS Forex must not promise profit, imply assured gains, or convert a successful evidence milestone into a future outcome promise.

## Loss Possible

AIOS Forex must disclose that losses can occur, including losses from spread, fees, slippage, execution delay, rejected orders, partial fills, market gaps, network failures, and user error.

## No Fixed Returns

AIOS Forex must not claim fixed return rates, predictable daily returns, or deterministic outcomes. Backtest, paper, demo, and live-micro evidence can support engineering readiness only when properly labeled.

## No Passive-Income Claim

AIOS Forex must not present itself as a passive-income product. Automation support must be framed as governed, reviewed, owner-controlled workflow support.

## Investment Advice Boundary

AIOS Forex must not claim to provide investment, legal, tax, or financial-services advice unless owner/legal review later approves exact public language. Default wording must identify the product as a governed automation and evidence tool, not an advisor.

## Broker Evidence Rule

No broker-result claim may be made without a broker receipt or owner-approved sanitized evidence record. Screenshots, logs, telemetry, and reports must not expose credentials, account identifiers, private broker data, or raw broker payloads.

## Realized Profit Rule

No realized profit claim may be made unless reconciled PnL accounts for spread, fees, slippage, rejection, partial fill, timing, and final broker-state evidence. Gross or preliminary output must be labeled as provisional.

## Repeat Attempt Rule

No repeated trade attempt may occur without owner review. A one-action stop rule must remain active for any future live-micro boundary. Demo or paper repeat loops must also preserve risk gates and evidence review.

## Max-Risk Concepts

Before release, AIOS Forex must define:

- per-action maximum loss concept.
- daily loss stop concept.
- instrument and unit boundary concept.
- approval window concept.
- stop-loss expectation.
- evidence bundle expectation.
- kill-switch confirmation.

These concepts are policy requirements until source implementation is separately approved.

## Daily-Loss Stop Concepts

Daily-loss stops must be visible, owner-governed, and fail-closed. A daily-loss stop must block further action until owner review, even if a strategy, dashboard, alert, or Vacation Mode state suggests continuation.

## Owner Acknowledgement Requirements

Before any user-facing release, the owner must approve exact disclosure language for:

- financial loss risk.
- no outcome certainty.
- no advice claim.
- live authority boundaries.
- support and escalation.
- shutdown and kill-switch expectations.

## External Review Requirement

External legal/compliance review is required before sale, public launch, store submission, financial-services claims, investment-advice claims, or commercial distribution. This policy does not replace that review.
